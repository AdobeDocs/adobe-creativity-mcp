# Prompts & workflows

Copy any prompt below into Claude to try it. The sections below are organized around the six skills that ship with Adobe for creativity — each section shows what that skill is good for and gives you prompts to try.  
Use these as starting points, then iterate in chat. Claude's strength is the back-and-forth: start with a rough direction and refine.

## 📸 Batch edit photos

**Skill:** `adobe-batch-edit-photos`

Apply consistent edits across a set of photos — exposure, color, straightening, cropping — so the whole batch looks like it belongs together.

`"Edit this batch of photos together. Even out the exposure, warm up the color temperature, straighten any that are slightly tilted, and crop to 4:5."`

**Variations:**

* "...and export at 2000px on the long edge as JPEG at 90% quality."
* "These were shot in mixed lighting — balance the white balance across all of them and lift the shadows slightly."
* "Match all of these to the look of the first photo."

## 💅 Retouch portraits

**Skill:** `adobe-retouch-portraits`

Drop in headshots and describe the look you want — balanced lighting, background blur, auto-straighten, and a portrait crop. Adobe for creativity applies the best Adobe tools for the task intelligently and in the right order, and with your input along the way, so you get polished, production-ready images.

`"Retouch these five headshots. Even skin tones, soften without erasing texture, brighten the eyes slightly, and match the color temperature across all of them."`

📹 [embed: "Batch headshot retouch" walkthrough] 

**Variations:**

* "Keep it subtle — just enough to look polished, not over-done."
* "Give me both a natural version and a higher-polish version of each."

## 📱 Resize photos and videos

**Skill:** `adobe-resize-photos-and-videos`

Upload a horizontal clip and ask to reformat it for YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, or any platform. Adobe tools handle cropping and resizing to the right dimensions, delivering a video that's ready to share.

`"Resize this photo for Instagram (1:1), Instagram story (9:16), and LinkedIn feed (1.91:1). Keep the subject centered in each crop."`

**Variations:**

* "Take this 16:9 video and give me versions for YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, and TikTok."
* "Give me a full social pack — every major platform size — for this hero image."

## ✂️ Quick video cuts

**Skill:** `adobe-edit-quick-cut`

Trim, reorder, and quick-cut video into short highlight or teaser clips.

`"Cut this 2-minute interview down to a 30-second highlight reel. Focus on the strongest quotes. Add simple fade transitions between clips."`

**Variations:**

* "Make a 15-second teaser version for Reels, with captions."
* "Give me a 45-second and a 90-second cut of the same content."

## 🎨 Design social variations

**Skill:** `adobe-create-social-variations`

Describe your campaign and choose from Adobe Express design examples surfaced right in the conversation. Update text and colors and then animate. With just a few short prompts, you get a finished, publish-ready asset.

`"Create four variations of this Instagram post. Keep the brand feel consistent but vary the background treatment and color palette."`

📹 [embed: "One post, four variations" walkthrough]

**Variations:**

* "Build variations for a full weekly posting schedule — seven versions, each slightly different."
* "Give me the same post with three different headlines so I can A/B test."

## 🖼️ Design from a template

**Skill:** `adobe-design-from-template`

Build new designs from a template, auto-filling your brand colors, fonts, logo, and content.

`"Make an Instagram story for my bakery's weekend sale using a template from my library. Use my brand colors and logo. Headline: 'Weekend Treats — 20% off all pastries Saturday & Sunday.'"`

**Variations:**

* "Same template, but for a Facebook event promo instead — different aspect ratio and CTA."
* "Use this template for all twelve products in this list, so each gets its own card."

## 🧩 Multi-step workflows (Cowork)

These prompts combine several skills (and sometimes other connectors) in a single request. They work best in Cowork, where Claude can access your local files and coordinate with Drive, Slack, and other tools in the same session.

### Shoot delivery package

"Take every photo in ~/Shoots/2026-04/raw. Run batch editing to even out the color, then retouch any that are portraits. Export two versions of each: web (1920px long edge, 85% JPEG) and print (full-resolution TIFF). Organize the results into `/web` and `/print` subfolders."

### Social launch pack

"Design a launch post for the new spring menu using my brand template. Then create four variations. Then resize each variation for Instagram feed, Instagram story, LinkedIn, and TikTok. Drop everything into ~/Desktop/Spring-Launch."

### Weekly content run

"Pull this week's approved copy from the 'Content Calendar' folder in my Google Drive. For each post, design a visual using my brand template and create three variations. Resize everything for Instagram and LinkedIn."

## Tips for writing good prompts

* **Describe the outcome, not the tool.** Claude picks the right skill. You describe what you want.
* **Include constraints up front.** Aspect ratio, file type, brand, platform — the more specific, the less back-and-forth.
* **Attach references.** A screenshot, mood image, or past asset helps Claude match your taste.
* **Iterate small.** It's easier to refine a rough output than to get a perfect one on the first try.
